On building a home server from spare parts

Three old laptops, a shelf in the closet, and a weekend. That’s the whole bill of materials. This entry is my notes on what worked, what caught fire (metaphorically), and why I’d do it again.

The first lesson: the hardware is the easy part. The hard part is deciding what not to run. Every self-hosted service is a small promise to your future self, and I broke about half of mine within a month.

What survived

A file sync node, a feed reader, and homelab-dash to watch them. Everything else got retired.

If you’re thinking about doing this: start with one service, run it for a month, then earn the second one.
